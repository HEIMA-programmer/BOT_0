### Short Answer (6)

1. **How does Dan define slow travel?**  
   **Answer:** He defines it as living in a place for an extended time, usually by renting a house or apartment, so you can experience daily life there.  
   **Explanation:** He contrasts this with moving quickly like a typical traveler.

2. **Why does Dan enjoy spending time at a local cafe?**  
   **Answer:** Because it helps him talk to local people and understand what life in the city is really like.  
   **Explanation:** He says this gives him a deeper sense of place.

3. **Why does Dan revisit places where he once lived?**  
   **Answer:** Because he has developed lifelong friendships there.  
   **Explanation:** He says one advantage of slow travel is forming deep connections.

4. **What does Dan say is the problem with staying only one month?**  
   **Answer:** He thinks people usually will not invest much time in you if your stay is that short.  
   **Explanation:** This idea is part of his theory about friendship.

5. **What is the first thing Dan recommends to someone interested in this lifestyle?**  
   **Answer:** He recommends connecting online with other people who are already doing it.  
   **Explanation:** He says this removes the mystery and makes the lifestyle feel achievable.

6. **Why does Dan think having a project matters?**  
   **Answer:** Because it helps you get engaged with the place instead of living passively.  
   **Explanation:** He says a project gives purpose and leads to deeper involvement.

### Short Answer (6)

1. **How does Dan distinguish slow travel from ordinary tourism?**  
   **Answer:** He says slow travel involves staying long enough to live locally, build routines, and form relationships instead of only passing through as a tourist.  
   **Explanation:** This contrast runs throughout his explanation.

2. **Why does Dan believe long stays are important for friendship?**  
   **Answer:** Because people are more willing to invest time and energy in you when they see that you are staying for months rather than just passing through.  
   **Explanation:** He gives three to four months as the likely threshold.

3. **What role does online community play in starting this lifestyle?**  
   **Answer:** It makes the idea feel realistic because you can see other people already doing it successfully.  
   **Explanation:** Dan says this removes the sense of mystery.

4. **Why does Dan repeatedly mention having a project in each location?**  
   **Answer:** Because projects help him connect with the place, meet people, and avoid living in a detached routine.  
   **Explanation:** He presents projects as the key to meaningful engagement.

5. **What kinds of projects does Dan suggest for non-business travelers?**  
   **Answer:** He suggests learning to surf, cooking local food, or learning the language.  
   **Explanation:** These are the examples he gives near the end.

6. **Why does Todd become convinced by Dan’s explanation?**  
   **Answer:** Because Dan presents slow travel as practical, achievable, and richer than ordinary tourism.  
   **Explanation:** Todd’s final reaction, “I’m sold,” shows that Dan’s reasoning worked.
