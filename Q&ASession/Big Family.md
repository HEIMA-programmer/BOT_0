## Beginner

### Multiple Choice (8)

1. **How many brothers does Sorie have?**  
   A. Three  
   B. Four  
   C. Five  
   D. Six  
   **Answer:** C  
   **Explanation:** Sorie says, “I have five brothers.”

2. **What is Sorie’s position among the children?**  
   A. She is the youngest  
   B. She is the oldest and only girl  
   C. She is the middle child  
   D. She has one older sister  
   **Answer:** B  
   **Explanation:** She says all five brothers are younger than her and she is “the only girl in the house.”

3. **How old is Sorie?**  
   A. 17

   C. 27  
   D. 32  
   **Answer:** C  
   **Explanation:** Sorie says, “I am 27.”

5. **How old was Sorie’s mother when she had her?**  
   A. 17  
   B. 19  
   C. 22  
   D. 27  
   **Answer:** A  
   **Explanation:** Sorie says, “She had me when she was 17 years old.”

6. **What does Sorie compare her childhood home to?**  
   A. A school  
   B. A farm  
   C. A zoo  
   D. A market  
   **Answer:** C  
   **Explanation:** She says, “it was very hectic, it was like a zoo.”

7. **What did Sorie say she actually wanted?**

   B. A big brother

8. **How many siblings does Mark mention having?**

### Short Answer (6)

1. **Why did Sorie feel her mother was almost like a sister?**  
   **Answer:** Because her mother had her at a very young age, so they were close in age.  
   **Explanation:** Sorie says her mother was still very young, which made their relationship feel different.

2. **What unusual memory does Sorie share from childhood?**  
   **Answer:** She remembers one brother chasing her with a big knife and the knife getting stuck in the door.  
   **Explanation:** She uses this story to show how hectic life in the family could be.

3. **How did growing up with brothers affect Sorie socially?**  
   **Answer:** It made her feel more comfortable around boys, and she had more male friends than female friends.  
   **Explanation:** She says this directly.

4. **Did Sorie strongly wish for a sister?**  
   **Answer:** No, she says she did not really crave having a sister.  
   **Explanation:** She clearly answers “not really.”

5. **How does Mark describe his family background?**  
   **Answer:** He says he comes from a very small family and grew up with only his sister.  
   **Explanation:** He contrasts his experience with Sorie’s big family.

6. **What lasting bond does Mark say he still has with his sister?**  
   **Answer:** He says they still have a strong sense of family connection even though they are very different.  
   **Explanation:** He explains that they can still speak to each other and share a family bond.

### Short Answer (6)

1. **How does Sorie describe the structure of her family?**

   **Explanation:** This is the central fact of her family background.

   **Answer:** It reveals that life was noisy, unpredictable, and sometimes wild.  
   **Explanation:** Her “zoo” comment and the knife story both support this idea.

3. **Why does Sorie feel more comfortable around males?**  
   **Answer:** Because she grew up surrounded by brothers and became used to that style of interaction.  
   **Explanation:** She says this upbringing made her feel more comfortable around boys than girls.

4. **What difference does Sorie notice between male and female friendships?**  
   **Answer:** She feels male friendships allow more direct honesty, while female friendships can require more emotional caution.  
   **Explanation:** That is how she contrasts the two in the conversation.

5. **How does Mark’s family experience differ from Sorie’s?**  
   **Answer:** He comes from a small family with just one sister, so his sibling experience was much less crowded and chaotic.  
   **Explanation:** He explicitly contrasts his background with hers.

6. **What is one example Mark gives of similarity with his sister despite their differences?**  
   **Answer:** He says they share the same sense of humor and laugh at the same things.  
   **Explanation:** He uses humor as proof of their underlying connection.
