## What Went Well

- **Clear division of tasks**: Each member owned specific modules (DB, UI, recording, API, forum, vocabulary).  
- **Collaboration**: PR reviews handled quickly, conflicts resolved.  
- **Innovation**: Team proposed gamification, accents, social features, and room functions.  
- **Delivery**: Listening Lab and Speaking Studio MVPs completed within sprint.  
- **Adaptability**: Team adjusted priorities (AI conversation moved earlier, forum merged with Academic Culture).  

---

## What Could Be Improved

- **Work smarter, not harder**: Time was limited, so prioritization and feature trade-offs were essential.  
- **Module logic**: Structured speaking scenarios lacked coherence; modules felt isolated.  
- **UI clarity**: Wording and tooltips confusing for new users; some buttons unclear.  
- **Engagement**: Vocabulary and listening modules still too exam-like, need more interactive/game elements.  
- **Accent diversity**: Current TTS limited; need official multi-accent sources.  
- **Cross-discipline coverage**: Forum should include guides for multiple majors (CS, Civil, Applied Math, Mechanical, etc.).
