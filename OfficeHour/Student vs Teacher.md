### Short Answer (6)

1. **Why does Abidemi enjoy being a student now?**  
   **Answer:** Because she enjoys learning and has less responsibility than she had as a teacher.  
   **Explanation:** She says she now only has to worry about herself.

2. **What does Abidemi miss about teaching?**  
   **Answer:** She misses teaching new things, sharing her opinions, leading discussions, and knowing students personally.  
   **Explanation:** She lists these as the parts of teaching she still values.

3. **How has becoming a student again changed Abidemi's view of teaching?**  
   **Answer:** It has made her appreciate students more and realize she had been too strict before.  
   **Explanation:** She says she would now be more lenient.

4. **Why does Abidemi think teachers should become students again?**  
   **Answer:** Because the experience helps them understand student pressure and become better teachers.  
   **Explanation:** She says every teacher should go back and become a student.

5. **What is Jeremy's similar experience?**  
   **Answer:** He taught after undergraduate study and later found that returning to school made him a more successful student.  
   **Explanation:** He compares his own journey to Abidemi's.

6. **What is one main idea of the conversation?**  
   **Answer:** Experiencing both roles, student and teacher, can deepen understanding and improve performance in each role.  
   **Explanation:** Both speakers say moving between the roles changed them positively.

### Short Answer (6)

1. **How has returning to school changed Abidemi's understanding of students?**

   **Explanation:** She now thinks she was too strict and would be more lenient.

   **Answer:** Because she misses helping others learn, sharing ideas, leading discussions, and building relationships with students.

   **Answer:** He shares that teaching first helped him appreciate student freedom and motivation, and returning to study later made him more successful.

   **Answer:** Because they better understand the purpose of class and take more personal responsibility for learning.

   **Answer:** Because it renews empathy and helps teachers judge workload, expectations, and classroom experience more fairly.  
   **Explanation:** Abidemi says she would now be more lenient and appreciative.
