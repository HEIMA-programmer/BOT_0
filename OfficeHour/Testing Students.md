### Short Answer (6)

1. **Why does Jana now see value in homework?**  
   **Answer:** Because homework motivates her to study and gives her structure when she does not study well on her own.  
   **Explanation:** She says she is terrible at studying independently, so homework helps.

2. **How does Peter redefine homework?**  
   **Answer:** He sees it as preparation, review, or continuation of the class rather than just extra work.  
   **Explanation:** Both speakers use these words to broaden the meaning of homework.

3. **Why does Peter think tests still exist in universities?**  
   **Answer:** Because institutions need grades and standards to show whether students passed or failed.  
   **Explanation:** He links testing to institutional requirements.

4. **What problem does Jana see with large end-of-semester tests?**  
   **Answer:** She finds them stressful, especially oral exams, and would rather have smaller tests over time.  
   **Explanation:** She says big final tests were unpleasant for her as a student.

5. **What concern does Peter raise about studying for tests?**  
   **Answer:** He worries that students may memorize information temporarily and forget it soon after the test.  
   **Explanation:** He describes students cramming the night before.

6. **What is one main issue discussed in the conversation?**  
   **Answer:** Whether homework and tests truly support learning or mainly serve institutional requirements.  
   **Explanation:** Both speakers weigh benefits against limitations.
