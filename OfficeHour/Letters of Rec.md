## Beginner

### Multiple Choice (8)

1. **How many letters of recommendation did Nydja need?**  
   A. One  
   B. Two  
   C. Three  
   D. Four  
   **Answer:** C  
   **Explanation:** She says, “I did have to do three letters of recommendation.”

2. **Whom did Nydja ask for recommendations?**  
   A. Only professors  
   B. Professors, an employer, and a family friend  
   C. Only relatives  
   D. Only classmates  
   **Answer:** B  
   **Explanation:** She lists professors, an employer, and a long-time family friend.

3. **How does Todd describe Nydja's group of recommenders?**  
   A. Weak  
   B. Diverse  
   C. Unreliable  
   D. Academic only  
   **Answer:** B  
   **Explanation:** Todd says she had “quite a diverse mix.”

4. **Why does Nydja say you should ask someone you trust for a reference?**  
   A. Because references are free  
   B. Because they may write something negative otherwise  
   C. Because schools require family members only  
   D. Because employers cannot write references  
   **Answer:** B  
   **Explanation:** She says people sometimes ask randomly and do not get the results they wanted.

5. **What does Nydja say applicants sometimes want recommenders to mention?**  
   A. Certain specific qualities  
   B. Their hobbies only  
   C. Their favorite classes  
   D. Their travel history  
   **Answer:** A  
   **Explanation:** She says sometimes you want them to mention certain things.

6. **Who sends the official transcript to the new school?**  
   A. The student  
   B. A professor  
   C. The undergraduate institution  
   D. A family friend  
   **Answer:** C  
   **Explanation:** She says the school where you earned your undergraduate degree sends it.

7. **Why do schools want transcripts sent directly?**  
   A. To save postage  
   B. Because they think students might change grades  
   C. Because students do not know the address  
   D. Because employers forbid it  
   **Answer:** B  
   **Explanation:** Nydja says schools do not trust students because they might “mess with” their grades.

8. **Which of these is NOT listed as part of the grad school application?**  
   A. Essays  
   B. Letter of intent  
   C. Official transcripts  
   D. Driving record  
   **Answer:** D  
   **Explanation:** Driving records are never mentioned in the conversation.

### Short Answer (6)

1. **Why was Nydja somewhat worried about recommendation letters?**  
   **Answer:** Because even when you trust the writer, you still wonder what they will say and which qualities they will choose to mention.  
   **Explanation:** She says applicants often think about what good things people will write.

2. **What kinds of people did Nydja choose for her recommendations?**  
   **Answer:** She chose a mix of professors, an employer, and a long-time family friend.  
   **Explanation:** This shows her recommendation sources were varied.

3. **Why are official transcripts sent directly to the school?**  
   **Answer:** Because schools want an official record and do not want students to alter it.  
   **Explanation:** Nydja says schools think students might mess with their grades.

4. **Besides transcripts and recommendation letters, what else is needed for grad school?**  
   **Answer:** Essays and a letter of intent are also required.  
   **Explanation:** She lists those items near the end of the conversation.

5. **How does Nydja distinguish an essay from a letter of intent?**  
   **Answer:** She says an essay can simply be a writing sample, while a letter of intent focuses on life goals and purpose.  
   **Explanation:** That is the exact contrast she makes.

6. **Why does Nydja dislike writing letters of intent?**  
   **Answer:** Because she feels life goals can change and the writing can seem contrived.  
   **Explanation:** She says she can sum it up briefly and dislikes turning it into a long paper.

### Short Answer (6)

1. **Why might an applicant choose recommenders from different backgrounds?**  
   **Answer:** Because different recommenders can speak to different strengths, such as academic ability, work performance, and personal character.  
   **Explanation:** Nydja's choices create a broader picture of who she is.

2. **What does the conversation reveal about trust in the application process?**  
   **Answer:** It shows that schools rely on official documents and trusted third parties because they want accurate information.  
   **Explanation:** This is why transcripts are sent directly and recommendations matter.

3. **Why might applicants give recommenders suggestions about what to write?**  
   **Answer:** To make sure important strengths or experiences are highlighted in the letter.  
   **Explanation:** Nydja says sometimes you want them to mention certain things.

4. **Why does Nydja find letters of intent uncomfortable to write?**  
   **Answer:** She feels they demand an overly fixed and extended statement about goals that may still be changing.  
   **Explanation:** She says her goals can change and the writing feels contrived.

5. **How is a letter of intent different from a general writing sample?**  
   **Answer:** A writing sample shows writing ability, while a letter of intent explains personal motivation, goals, and purpose.  
   **Explanation:** That distinction is made directly in the conversation.

6. **What broader challenge of graduate applications appears in this dialogue?**  
   **Answer:** Applicants must not only prove their past record but also present a convincing future direction.  
   **Explanation:** The transcript combines academic documents with statements about life goals.
